| 4.0 | 20.00 | 0.50 | 1.99 | 0.00 | 34.83 | 24.88 | 3 | 3.96 | Intermediate | 32.985 | 21.567 | 28.729 | 1.3 | 1.2 | 0.8 | 1.077 | 1.038 | 1.038 | 1 | 1 | 1 | 279.56 | 93.19 |

**Key Formulas Used:**
- **Intermediate Shear Failure:** qult = (5/6) C N''c Sc dc ic + q (N''q − 1) Sq dq iq + 0.5 B γ N''γ Sγ dγ iγ W'
- **C = 0** (no cohesion — gravels)
- **Shape Factors:** Sc=1.3, Sq=1.2, Sγ=0.8 (for circular/square, from Table 2 of IS 6403)
- **Depth Factors:** dc, dq, dγ calculated per IS 6403
- **Inclination Factors:** ic = iq = iγ = 1 (no inclination)
- **W' = 0.5** (water table correction factor)
- **Factor of Safety = 3**

### 6b. Detailed Worked Example (at 2.475m depth)

- γ = γsat = 1.99 t/m³
- γb = γsat − 1 = 0.99 t/m³
- q = γb × d = 0.99 × 2.475 = 2.450 t/m²
- B = 20m (raft diameter)
- qult = 2.450 × (20.489 − 1) × 1.2 × 1.023 × 1 + 0.5 × 20 × 1.99 × 26.866 × 0.6 × 1.023 × 1 × 0.5
- qult = 222.80 t/m²
- **SBC = 222.80 / 3 = 74.27 t/m²**

## 13. Inconsistencies and Data Flags

### FLAG 1: Bore Termination Depth Contradiction
- **Bore log page (Page 13):** States "Bore terminated at 15m depth from the existing ground level"
- **Summary and all other sections:** Consistently state the bore hole is 10m depth
- **Assessment:** Likely a template error on the bore log page. The 10m depth is used everywhere else.

### FLAG 2: Natural Moisture Content Near Zero
- **Value:** 0.001% for gravels
- **Assessment:** Extremely low. While gravels can have low moisture content, 0.001% is essentially zero. Could be a reporting artifact or the material was truly dry (consistent with no GWT encountered).

### FLAG 3: Compressive Strength Value in Geo Properties Table
- **Page 20 shows:** 1818 MPa in the engineering properties table
- **Page 46 (Rock parameters):** UCS = 42 MPa for hard rock
- **Assessment:** The 1818 value on page 20 appears to be a Bulk Modulus or other parameter misplaced; 42 MPa for the rock UCS is the correct value per the detailed rock testing section.

### FLAG 4: Shape Factor Sγ Inconsistency
- **Section 5.3 table header shows:** Sγ = 0.8
- **Worked example (Section 4.1, page 33):** Uses Sγ = 0.6 in the calculation: `0.5 × 20 × 1.99 × 26.866 × 0.6 × 1.023`
- **Assessment:** The worked example uses Sγ=0.6 (for rectangular) but the tabular summary shows Sγ=0.8 (for circular). The circular value of 0.8 appears in the header, but 0.6 is what makes the arithmetic work for the reported qult=222.80. **This is a genuine inconsistency between the table header and the actual computation.**

### FLAG 5: Water Table Correction W'=0.5 Despite No GWT
- **GWT:** Not encountered up to 10m
- **W' used:** 0.5 (implies water at or above foundation level)
- **Assessment:** Conservative assumption. For no-GWT scenarios, W'=1.0 would be appropriate. Using W'=0.5 halves the γNγ term, reducing SBC. This provides a safety margin but may underestimate the actual capacity.

### FLAG 6: SPT N-values vs Bore Log N-values
- **Bore log (Page 13):** Shows N=50, 52, 53, 54, 56
- **Lab results table (Page 26):** Shows Relative Density = 55%, 57%, 59%, 60%, 61%
- **Shear parameters (Page 27):** Shows N=50, 52, 53, 54, 56
- **Assessment:** Consistent across sections — no flag here.

### FLAG 7: Rock Density vs Specific Gravity
- **Rock Density:** 3.400 gm/cc
- **Rock Specific Gravity:** 2.85
- **Assessment:** Density > Specific Gravity × γw is unusual. Specific gravity of 2.85 suggests density should be ≤ 2.85 gm/cc (if no voids). A density of 3.4 gm/cc with Sp.Gr of 2.85 implies measurement inconsistency or the rock contains heavy minerals.

### FLAG 8: Only One Borehole for Wind Turbine Foundation
- **Assessment:** A single borehole for a 20m diameter wind turbine raft foundation provides limited spatial coverage. Industry practice typically requires multiple boreholes to characterize variability.
